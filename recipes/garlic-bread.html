Garlic Bread


Description

Garlic bread consists of bread, often a baguette or sour dough like a ciabatta, which is spread with a mixture of butter and fresh garlic before being baked or broiled. Variants may include additional ingredients like cheese, herbs, or olive oil. The bread is then either grilled or broiled until toasted and the garlic flavor is pronounced, creating a savory, fragrant side dish or appetizer. Perfect for pairing with pasta dishes, soups, or salads, garlic bread is beloved for its crispy texture and buttery garlic flavor.

  • 1 baguette or loaf of Italian bread
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 to 6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • Optional toppings: shredded mozzarella cheese, dried oregano, chili flakes

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cut the baguette or loaf of bread in half horizontally.
  3. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter, minced garlic, parsley, salt, and pepper. If using, mix in the grated Parmesan cheese.
  4. Spread the butter mixture evenly over the cut sides of the bread.
  5. If desired, sprinkle with additional toppings like shredded mozzarella, oregano, or chili flakes.
  6. Place on a baking sheet, cut side up, and bake in the preheated oven for 10-15 minutes, or until the edges are golden and the butter is bubbling.
  7. For a crispier top, broil for an additional 1-2 minutes, watching carefully to avoid burning.
  8. Remove from the oven, slice, and serve warm.
